(iv) Information collected through cookies, web beacons, or device identifiers, as described below: Cookies: Cookies are small text files that we send to your computer to uniquely identify your browser, convey information to us on how you use the Services and track your usage of the Services. Other companies that provide analytics tools and place advertising on our site also have the ability to place cookies on your computer. We may use “session cookies” and “persistent cookies.” A “session cookie” is temporary and disappears when you close your browser. A “persistent cookie” remains on your hard drive after you close your browser, and may be used on subsequent visits to the Service. Persistent cookies may be removed by configuring your browser’s functionality to remove cookies, and you can reset your browser to refuse all cookies or notify you when a cookie is sent. Some features of the Services may not function properly if cookies are disabled. At this time, we do not recognize “Do Not Track” signals sent from web browsers. Web beacons: Our web pages or email messages may send a small graphic image called a web beacon that allows us to collect certain information such as the IP address of the computer receiving the web beacon, the type of browser requesting the web beacon, and the time the web beacon was viewed. We may use web beacons to count visitors to our Website, to monitor our how our users navigate our Website, and in emails to count how many were opened by the recipients. Web beacons often work together with cookies and in many cases, declining or blocking cookies will impair the web beacons associated with those cookies. Device Identifiers: When you access or use the Services using a mobile device, tablet or streaming media device, we may access, collect, monitor, or remotely store one or more device identifiers. Device identifiers are small data files or similar data structures similar to cookies and are found on many mobile devices, tablets and streaming media devices. They are used to for analytics and optimization purposes and to make online advertising more relevant for the user. To opt out of interest-based ads from us in connection with a device identifier, you may configure the appropriate setting on your mobile device, tablet or streaming media device, which is often found under “ads” or “privacy” in the device settings. (v) Location Data. When you access or use the Services, we may access, collect, monitor, or remotely store location data, which may include GPS coordinates or similar information identifying the location of your device. Location data conveys to us information about how you browse and use the Services and allows us to provide localized features and content.
